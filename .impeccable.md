## Design Context

### Users
Staff and employees of a plumbing supplies business. Used at a desk, daily — both for quick lookups and longer data-entry sessions. These are professional workers moving fast through real tasks: checking supplier details, updating product pricing, managing customer accounts. They are not here to be delighted by the UI — they're here to get work done and move on. The interface should get out of their way.

### Brand Personality
**Professional. Clean. Efficient.**

The emotional register is confidence and quiet authority — the feeling of a sharp, well-made tool in capable hands. Not cold or sterile, but purposeful and no-fuss. Every element earns its place.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference points**: Linear (dark, minimal, sharp), Vercel Dashboard (clean tables, excellent spacing, nothing unnecessary). Both are hallmarks of B2B product design done right — high information density without visual noise.

**Theme**: Both light and dark mode, equally intentional. Dark mode is the night-shift workspace; light mode is the daylight desk. Neither is the fallback.

**Palette**: Neutral — dark navy or deep slate for dark mode surfaces, clean white/cool light grey for light mode. Blue accents are approved and expected. No purple, no gradients, no decorative color.

**Typography**: Sharp and precise. A clean grotesque or geometric sans-serif that feels editorial rather than friendly. Nothing rounded, bubbly, or playful. Type hierarchy should make tables instantly scannable — labels clearly subordinate to values.

**Anti-references**: Generic SaaS templates, purple-to-blue gradients, rounded bubbly components, emoji-style decorations, cards-on-cards layouts, hero metric layouts with glowing numbers.

### Design Principles
1. **Data first** — Every layout decision serves legibility of tables and data. Nothing competes with the content for attention.
2. **Zero ornamentation** — If a visual element doesn't help a user complete a task faster or more accurately, it does not exist.
3. **Sharp and precise** — Crisp edges, tight-but-breathable spacing, clear type hierarchy. Not rounded, not decorative, not warm.
4. **Confident density** — Enough information on screen to reduce navigation. Staff shouldn't scroll to find what they need, but the layout should never feel cramped.
5. **Equally intentional modes** — Light and dark are both first-class. Design both; don't theme one as an afterthought of the other.
